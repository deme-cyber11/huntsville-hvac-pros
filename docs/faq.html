HVAC FAQ for Huntsville Homeowners

Frequently Asked Questions

AC repair costs in Huntsville typically range from $75–$500 for common repairs like refrigerant recharge, capacitor replacement, or contactor replacement. Compressor replacement can run $800–$2,000+ depending on the unit. Coil replacement or full system failure can exceed $3,000, at which point replacement often makes more financial sense.

We always provide a written diagnosis and price quote before starting any work. You decide whether to proceed — no pressure.

Use the "5,000 rule": multiply the repair cost by the age of your unit in years. If the result exceeds $5,000, replacement is usually the smarter investment.

Example: $800 repair × 10-year-old unit = $8,000 → lean toward replacement.
$200 repair × 5-year-old unit = $1,000 → repair makes sense.

Also consider: if your system is over 15 years old, uses R-22 refrigerant (now discontinued), or has had multiple major repairs in the past 3 years, replacement is almost always the right call.

In Alabama's hot, humid climate, proper sizing is critical — and it's more complex than "one ton per 400 sq ft." As a rough estimate, plan for approximately 1 ton of cooling per 400–600 sq ft, but many factors affect this:

  • Ceiling height (vaulted ceilings need more capacity)
  • Insulation quality
  • Number and size of windows
  • Shade and sun exposure
  • Number of occupants
  • Duct condition and layout

We perform a Manual J load calculation on every installation — it's the only accurate way to size a system. An oversized AC won't dehumidify properly (a major issue in Alabama), and an undersized system will run constantly and fail early.

We recommend two professional tune-ups per year:

  • Spring (March–April): Pre-season AC inspection before Huntsville's brutal summer heat arrives
  • Fall (October–November): Heating system check before cold snaps hit North Alabama

Alabama's long summers (AC runs May through October) put extreme wear on systems. Regular maintenance extends system life by 3–5 years and helps you avoid emergency breakdowns when temps hit 95°F.

Yes — Alabama requires HVAC contractors to hold a license through the Alabama Licensing Board for General Contractors (ALBGC). This includes installation, replacement, and repair of heating, cooling, and ventilation systems.

Always verify a contractor's license before hiring. Unlicensed HVAC work can void your equipment warranty and create liability issues if something goes wrong.

  • Low refrigerant — often from a slow leak; the system runs but can't cool effectively
  • Frozen evaporator coil — usually caused by dirty filter or low refrigerant; restricts airflow
  • Dirty condenser coils — outdoor unit can't reject heat properly in Alabama's humidity
  • Failed capacitor — common in our climate; causes the compressor or fan motor to stop
  • Compressor failure — the most expensive failure; often means replacement
  • Duct leaks — cooled air escaping into the attic or crawlspace
