5 AC Prep Steps Every Huntsville Homeowner Should Do Before Summer

By Huntsville HVAC Pros | Expert HVAC advice for Huntsville, Alabama homeowners.

If you've lived in Huntsville for more than one summer, you know the drill: June arrives, the humidity climbs past 90%, daytime highs hit 91°F or more, and your AC becomes the most important appliance in your home. It runs for months straight without a break.

The problem is that most homeowners don't think about their HVAC system until it fails — usually at 9pm on the hottest day of the year. By then, the only question is how long until a tech can get there.

A little preparation in April or May can be the difference between a comfortable summer and an emergency call. Here's what we recommend for every Huntsville homeowner before summer hits.

1. Replace Your Air Filter (and Know Your Size)

This is the single most impactful thing you can do yourself. A clogged air filter restricts airflow, which causes your AC to work harder, cool less effectively, and — in Huntsville's humidity — freeze the evaporator coil. A frozen coil means no cooling and a potential repair call.

Change your filter every 1–3 months during heavy use (May through October in Alabama). Check the size printed on the edge of your old filter and stock up on replacements. MERV 8–11 filters are the sweet spot: they catch particles effectively without overly restricting airflow.

2. Clean Around Your Outdoor Unit

Your outdoor condenser unit needs at least 2 feet of clearance on all sides to dissipate heat effectively. In Alabama's spring growth season, grass, weeds, and shrubs can quickly encroach. Also check for debris — leaves, cottonwood fluff, and grass clippings pile up in the fins and restrict airflow.

You can gently rinse the fins with a garden hose (spraying outward, not inward) to remove debris. Never use a pressure washer — you'll bend the fins and reduce efficiency. If they're heavily clogged, call us for a professional coil cleaning.

3. Schedule a Professional Spring Tune-Up

A DIY check is a good start, but it can't replace what a certified technician catches during a professional tune-up. Our spring AC service includes:

  • Refrigerant level check and leak inspection
  • Electrical connection inspection (loose connections are a fire risk)
  • Capacitor and contactor testing — these are the #1 cause of summer AC failures
  • Evaporator and condenser coil cleaning
  • Condensate drain clearing — a clogged drain can cause water damage or shut down your system
  • Blower motor inspection and lubrication
  • Thermostat calibration

Our spring tune-ups in Huntsville typically run $89–$149 and often catch $300–$1,500 in potential problems before they become failures. Schedule early — we get booked fast once May arrives.

4. Test Your Thermostat Before You Need It

Set your thermostat to cool mode and drop the temperature 5 degrees below the current indoor reading. Within a few minutes, you should hear the outdoor unit kick on and feel cool air from the vents. If it doesn't respond, check your thermostat batteries first, then the circuit breaker. If those are fine, call us — thermostat, wiring, or control board issues are easier and cheaper to fix in April than in a crisis.

Consider upgrading to a smart thermostat like the Ecobee or Nest if you haven't already. In Alabama's climate, smart setback scheduling can reduce cooling costs by 15–20% — and you can check on your system remotely while you're on vacation.

5. Check Your Vents and Ductwork

Walk through your home and confirm all supply and return vents are open and unobstructed. Blocked vents create pressure imbalances that stress your system and reduce efficiency. Don't close vents in unused rooms — this is a common myth. It actually causes duct pressure problems and can damage your equipment.

If some rooms are consistently hotter than others, or if your utility bills seem high for your system's age, your ductwork may have leaks. Duct sealing is one of the most cost-effective home improvements in Alabama — leaky ducts can waste 20–30% of the air you're paying to cool.

When to Call the Professionals

If you find any of these during your spring check, call Huntsville HVAC Pros for same-day service:

  • Ice forming on the refrigerant lines or outdoor unit
  • Unusual noises from the indoor or outdoor unit
  • Burning or electrical smell when the system runs
  • AC not reaching the set temperature even after running for an hour
  • Significantly higher electric bills than last spring
